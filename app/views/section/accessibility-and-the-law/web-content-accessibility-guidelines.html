Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.1

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) are a list of criteria your website or mobile application need to meet in order to be legally compliant.

In order for your website or application to be legally compliant, it needs to meet the level of double A. This is outlined in the accessibility regulations.

Understanding WCAG

WCAG 2.1 is a list of critera. Each one is specifically designed to consider a particular need, but will fit into one of the following 4 categories:

  1. Perceivable
  2. Operable
  3. Understandable
  4. Robust

The criteria make up 3 levels. A, double A and triple A. The levels build on one another, so to be double A compliant you need to meet all of the A and double A criteria. For A and double A there are around 50 criteria in total.

You can find a list of all the A and double A criteria you need to meet in the W3C WCAG 2.1 QuickRef guide .
